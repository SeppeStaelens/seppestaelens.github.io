About Me

I am a passionate PhD student at the University of Cambridge, deeply fascinated by the enigmatic mysteries of black holes and the profound insights they offer into the fabric of space and time. My love for the subject of General Relativity fuels my dedication to unraveling its complexities and exploring its implications.

Currently, I am engaged in groundbreaking research on numerical relativity under the expert guidance of Professor Ulrich Sperhake. Through computational simulations and mathematical modeling, I strive to deepen our understanding of black holes and their behavior within the framework of General Relativity.

Research

My research interests include mathematical modeling, computational physics, and numerical methods.
