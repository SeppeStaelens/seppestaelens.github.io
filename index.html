About Me

I am a PhD student at the University of Cambridge, Department of Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics (DAMTP). I have always been passionate about research at the intersection of theoretical physics and astrophysics, e.g. black holes, gravitational waves and cosmology.

I have obtained Bachelor degrees (2020) in Mathematics and Physics (as part of the TWIN-programma at KU Leuven, Belgium), after which I pursued Master's degrees in Theoretical Physics (2022) and Astronomy and Astrophysics (2023). For the latter, I have undertaken a year-long Erasmus exchange to the Radboud University, Nijmegen, where I conducted research under supervision of Professor Peter Jonker and Professor Gijs Nelemans.

Research

Currently, I am engaged in research on black holes and boson stars, employing the tools of numerical relativity under the expert guidance of Professor Ulrich Sperhake. Through computational simulations and mathematical modeling, I strive to deepen our understanding of these compact objects and their behavior within the framework of General Relativity.

Past research projects have included work on black hole photon rings, hypercompact stellar clusters and the gravitational wave background sourced by white dwarf binaries.
